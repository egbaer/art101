Studio Project One

Part One

So, basically, I am in an art class right now (2022 fall quarter). I really love being able to see others art as they work on the same projects that I do. It is neat to see their thought processes behind them. Instead of commenting on every piece through Canvas and looking like a weirdo, I decided to make this. Because this is less creepy I guess? It is probably super creepy, lol. This page is presented in the same order and with the same comments as in the real class. You too can get the experience of taking an introductory art class at my school but only observing and not doing the work. Comments that are color are reflections from me that aren't on the actual page. In the interest of balancing both privacy and giving credit to the artists, I will be using a different initialism for each name. These will be consistent so you can identify what works a person has made, but you won't know their actual name. Hopefully, that is okay. If anyone manages to find this and wants me to remove their section(s) I will.
Everything I make/put here is done with good intentions.

Comments:

CVC

I really find it compelling how your poses for the character designs have some form of sequence, specifically showing a kicking animation. The art made for the project is quite dynamic and does not feel like its a static image. It is visually unique in a sense that the feeling behind the character's motion is seen through the sketch style and its details with the character moving in a sort of 3D space in its art sequence rather than a fixed 2D image.

NZ

What makes this visually unique is the movement of the characters, as well as the bright colors used within the work. I was first attracted to this design because the vibrant colors stood out to me. The color of the first character's skirt (the bright blue) contrasts with the dark blue background and it makes the eyes more attracted to the character. Similarly with the second design, against the plain grey background, the character is dressed in a bright red so it appeals to the eyes. I also really enjoyed seeing the variation of movement in both character designs, from standing to kicking to holding a sword, it was really compelling to me.
